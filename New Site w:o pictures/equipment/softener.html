Water Softener

Hard water is one of the most common problems in Southern California — and one of the most noticeable inside your home.

What is hard water?

Hard water contains high levels of calcium and magnesium. When water dries, these minerals stay behind — creating white spots, scale, and buildup.

In Los Angeles area, water hardness varies significantly:

  • Burbank: ~15–17 grains per gallon
  • Los Angeles: ~10–18 GPG
  • Ventura County: up to 60–80+ GPG

What problems does it cause?

White spots & scale

Builds up on faucets, glass, and appliances.

Dry skin & hair

Leaves residue that affects skin and hair.

Soap doesn’t work well

Hard water reduces soap efficiency.

Damage to plumbing

Scale builds up inside pipes and heaters.

What does a water softener do?

A water softener removes calcium and magnesium through an ion exchange process, replacing them with sodium.

This prevents scale and makes water feel softer.

How is it installed?

The system is installed on the main water line, so it treats all water in the home.

It requires:

  • Connection to the main line
  • Drain line for regeneration
  • Electrical outlet

Every home is different

Water softeners are sized based on:

  • Water hardness
  • Number of people in the home
  • Water usage

That’s why we always test your water before recommending a system.
