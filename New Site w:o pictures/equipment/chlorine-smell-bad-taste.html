Chlorine Smell & Bad Taste

If your water smells like bleach or has a chemical taste, you are noticing chlorine or chloramine — disinfectants that are intentionally added to city water.

These chemicals are used to kill bacteria and make water safe to deliver across long distances. Without them, water systems would not meet safety standards.

But the same reason they work is also why many people notice them — chlorine is designed to kill living organisms, and that affects how water feels, smells, and tastes inside your home.

What is in Los Angeles water?

The Los Angeles Department of Water and Power (LADWP) uses chloramine, which is a combination of chlorine and ammonia.

It is used instead of pure chlorine because it stays active longer in the water system and protects water as it travels through pipes.

Source: LADWP Water Quality Reports

How much chlorine is allowed?

According to U.S. EPA standards, disinfectants like chlorine or chloramine are allowed up to:

  • 4.0 mg/L (milligrams per liter)
  • which is the same as 4.0 ppm (parts per million)

These levels are considered safe for drinking water.

However, many people can smell or taste chlorine at levels far below the maximum allowed limit.

Why you notice it in your home

  • Hot water makes chlorine smell stronger
  • Standing water overnight increases taste and odor
  • Pipes and plumbing can intensify the reaction
  • Some people are more sensitive to chlorine than others

This is why the same city water can feel very different from one home to another.

Is chlorine good or bad?

Chlorine is necessary for public safety. It prevents bacteria and contamination in the water supply.

But it is not something your body needs — it is simply a disinfectant.

Once water reaches your home, the job of chlorine is already done. At that point, many homeowners choose to remove it to improve taste, smell, and overall water quality.

How to test your water

  • Home chlorine test strips (quick check)
  • City water quality reports
  • Professional in-home testing

What usually improves this problem

  • Carbon filtration (removes chlorine & chloramine)
  • Whole house filtration systems
  • Reverse osmosis drinking systems

The right solution depends on whether you want to improve drinking water only or the entire home.
