Chlorine Smell & Bad Taste

If your water smells like bleach or has a chemical taste, you are most likely noticing chlorine or chloramine — disinfectants that are intentionally added to city water.

These chemicals are used to kill bacteria and keep water safe as it travels through pipes. However, they often affect taste, smell, and overall water experience inside the home.

What is actually in Los Angeles water?

The Los Angeles Department of Water and Power (LADWP) uses chloramine as a disinfectant to keep water safe while it travels through the system. [oai_citation:0‡LADWP](https://www.ladwp.com/who-we-are/water-system/las-drinking-water-quality-report/la-water-sources-2022?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Chloramine is a combination of chlorine and ammonia. It stays in the water longer than chlorine and helps prevent bacteria growth inside pipelines. [oai_citation:1‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chloramination?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

How much chlorine is allowed?

According to U.S. EPA standards, disinfectants like chlorine or chloramine are allowed in drinking water at levels up to:

  • 4.0 mg/L (ppm) maximum allowed level

This is the legal safety limit used across the United States. [oai_citation:2‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drinking_water_quality_in_the_United_States?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

In Los Angeles, typical levels are usually much lower, but still enough for many people to notice taste and smell.

Why does water smell or taste like chlorine?

  • Chloramine reacts with organic material inside plumbing
  • It forms byproducts that create a strong chemical smell
  • Hot water can intensify the odor
  • Older pipes can make the taste worse

LADWP notes that a “bleach-like” smell is commonly linked to disinfectants interacting inside the plumbing system. [oai_citation:3‡LADWP](https://www.ladwp.com/who-we-are/water-system/water-quality/improving-water-quality-home?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Is it safe?

Yes — city water is regulated and must meet federal and state standards.

However, “safe” does not always mean pleasant:

  • taste and odor are not regulated the same way
  • people are sensitive to chlorine smell
  • some homeowners prefer to reduce exposure

How to test your water

  • Free test from local water company (LADWP)
  • Home chlorine test strips
  • Professional water testing

LADWP provides water quality reports and offers testing options for residents. [oai_citation:4‡LADWP](https://www.ladwp.com/sites/default/files/documents/2020_Drinking_Water_Quality_Report.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

What usually improves this problem

  • Carbon filtration (removes chlorine & chloramine)
  • Whole house filtration systems
  • Drinking water RO systems

The right solution depends on whether the issue is only taste or affects the entire house.
